### 1. **BofA Calls for Three Fed Rate Hikes in 2026 — Most Hawkish Major Bank Forecast**

**Source:** Fortune ([Original Article](https://fortune.com/2026/06/22/fed-rate-hikes-outlook-sticky-inflation-kevin-warsh-job-growth-oil-prices/)) / TheStreet

Bank of America published a sweeping revision to its U.S. rate outlook, now forecasting three quarter-point hikes in September, October, and December 2026, lifting the fed funds target to 4.25%–4.50% from the current 3.5%–3.75%. Previously, BofA had expected rates to remain on hold for the full year — the reversal represents a 75 basis point hawkish shift.

BofA's case rests on three pillars: Kevin Warsh's price-stability-first tone at his inaugural FOMC press conference, a dot plot in which half of FOMC participants projected at least one hike before year-end, and continued labor market resilience (initial jobless claims persistently below 220K).
