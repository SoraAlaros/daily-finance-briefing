## 🌍 Global Markets & Key Events

### 1. **U.S. and Iran Sign 14-Point Peace Deal, Strait of Hormuz Naval Blockade Lifted**

**Source:** Bloomberg / Gulf News ([Original Article](https://gulfnews.com/amp/story/business/energy/after-111-days-of-war-iran-oil-sales-set-to-roar-as-sanctions-relief-could-unleash-billions-reshape-global-energy-markets-1.500578128))

The United States and Iran formally signed a 14-point memorandum of understanding on Thursday, ending over 100 days of armed conflict. The deal calls for Iran to dilute its stockpile of highly enriched uranium, while the U.S. immediately lifted its naval blockade of the Strait of Hormuz and waived oil export sanctions.

Iran's first shipment of approximately 3.8 million barrels of crude oil successfully transited the Strait hours after the agreement was signed. The deal also provides for a reduction of U.S. military assets from the region and partial unfreezing of Iranian assets, though the 60-day toll-free passage arrangement leaves long-term sustainability in question.

### 3. **Trump Confirms Intel-Apple Chipmaking Deal; Intel Surges to Record**

**Source:** CNBC / Bloomberg ([Original Article](https://www.cnbc.com/amp/2026/06/18/trump-intel-apple-chip-design-deal.html))

Global bond yields have risen sharply amid persistent inflation, resilient growth and elevated fiscal deficits driving issuance. Goldman notes that even if yields move meaningfully higher from here, the carry cushion on current bonds is thick enough to avoid negative total returns over a 12-month horizon. Term premiums — the compensation investors demand for holding long-duration bonds — are expanding and may continue to do so.

### PIMCO: 10-Year Yields in Fair-Value Range; Fixed Income Attractiveness Restored

**Source:** PIMCO Cyclical Outlook — "Seeking Stability" ([Link](https://www.pimco.com/us/en/insights/seeking-stability))

**Key view:** **PIMCO places the U.S. 10-year Treasury yield's fair cyclical range at 3.75%-4.75%, with the current 4.43% level sitting near the midpoint — making fixed income the most attractively priced asset class in nearly a decade on a risk-adjusted basis.**

PIMCO favors TIPS for the inflation-adjustment buffer embedded in their yields. If recession risks escalate, markets could price in aggressive Fed cuts, providing significant duration upside. Conversely, if inflation reaccelerates, TIPS offer direct protection. The firm views the current moment as a rare opportunity to lock in elevated real yields across the yield curve.

### J.P. Morgan / PIMCO: Bond Market Is Misjudging Slowdown Risk; New Fixed-Income Regime Underway

**Source:** Advisor Perspectives ([Link](https://www.advisorperspectives.com/articles/2026/03/30/jpmorgan-pimco-bond-market-misjudging-slowdown-risk))

**Key view:** **Both J.P. Morgan and PIMCO warned earlier this year that the bond market was underpricing economic slowdown risk, and with the Fed now signaling a possible hike, they argue that fixed income is entering a structurally different regime driven by supply shocks, elevated yields, and shifting stock-bond correlations.**

In this new regime, the traditional inverse stock-bond correlation that underpinned 60/40 portfolio theory may not hold reliably. Persistent fiscal deficits and elevated Treasury supply could keep long-end term premiums structurally elevated, even as short rates eventually turn lower. Both firms recommend investors actively manage duration rather than passively holding benchmark exposures.
