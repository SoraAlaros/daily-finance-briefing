## 🌍 Global Markets & Key Events

### 1. **Trump Declines to Renew USMCA for 16 Years, Triggering Annual Reviews**

**Source:** CNBC / Washington Post ([Original Article](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/01/trump-usmca-canada-mexico-trade-treaty.html))

The Trump administration allowed the July 1 deadline to renew the US-Mexico-Canada Agreement for another 16-year term to pass without a commitment, opting instead for a framework of annual reviews. The pact remains in force for another decade, but major provisions will be subject to renegotiation each year, injecting fresh uncertainty into North America's $2 trillion-a-year trade relationship.

Industries most exposed — automakers, dairy, and agricultural sectors — face the prospect of ongoing disruptions to long-term planning. Analysts warn that persistent uncertainty could prompt businesses to reassess supply chains and redirect investment away from cross-border projects.

### 2. **Fed Chair Warsh Debuts on Global Stage at ECB Sintra Forum: Inflation "Too High," No July Rate Signal**

**Source:** CNBC ([Original Article](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/01/kevin-warsh-ecb-forum-live-updates.html))

Federal Reserve Chair Kevin Warsh made his first major international appearance at the European Central Bank's Forum on Central Banking in Sintra, Portugal, appearing alongside ECB President Christine Lagarde, Bank of England Governor Andrew Bailey, and Bank of Canada Governor Tiff Macklem. Warsh stated prices remain "too high" and that the Fed would make a rate decision in four weeks, but declined to indicate direction, saying only that policymakers "will have a good debate."

Warsh also addressed the AI economic shock, arguing that rising capital expenditure signals an expected supply-side expansion — with "huge implications for monetary policy." Central bank chiefs from multiple countries joined Warsh in opposing forward guidance, a shift that complicates market forecasting of the Fed's path.

### 3. **ADP June Jobs Miss: Private Payrolls Add 98K, Below Forecast**

**Source:** U.S. News / ADP ([Original Article](https://www.usnews.com/news/national-news/articles/2026-07-01/adp-job-growth-slows-in-june-as-98-000-positions-added))

ADP's National Employment Report showed private-sector employers added only 98,000 jobs in June, trailing the 118,000 consensus estimate and down from 122,000 in May. Education and healthcare led gains (+48K), while ADP Chief Economist Nela Richardson noted it is "taking people longer to find work" amid signs of labor supply constraints in select industries.

The same day's JOLTS release showed May job openings unchanged at 7.6 million, slightly above expectations, with the openings-to-unemployed ratio ticking up to 1.03. The soft ADP reading set a cautious tone ahead of Thursday's official June Nonfarm Payrolls report, the key event of the holiday-shortened week.

### 4. **China June Manufacturing PMI Returns to Expansion, Beat by AI-Linked Exports**

**Source:** National Bureau of Statistics of China ([Original Article](https://www.stats.gov.cn/english/PressRelease/202606/t20260601_1963851.html))

China's official NBS Manufacturing PMI rose to 50.3 in June, exceeding the 50.1 consensus and extending the expansion streak to three consecutive months. New export orders returned to growth (50.1 vs. prior 48.6) as AI-related high-tech manufacturing shipments accelerated, while output accelerated and purchasing activity rebounded. Caixin Manufacturing PMI came in at 51.8, slightly below the prior 52.2 but remaining solidly in expansion territory for a sixth straight month.

Employment remained subdued (48.4) and domestic demand continued to lag, underscoring that China's recovery remains uneven. The better-than-expected PMI data eased concerns about a second-half slowdown and supported risk sentiment in Asian equity markets.

### 8. **Gold Nears Worst Quarterly Performance in Decades as Rate Fears Mount**

**Source:** Bloomberg / Reuters

Gold traded near $4,030 per ounce — close to an eight-month low — after tumbling roughly 11% in Q2 2026, its worst quarterly decline in decades. Fed Chair Warsh's refusal to signal rate cuts reinforced hawkish sentiment.

Analysts note that gold's collapse coincides with a reversal of the "dollar debasement" trade that had pushed the metal to all-time highs in 2025. Near-term upside remains capped unless the Fed pivots or geopolitical risk escalates unexpectedly.
