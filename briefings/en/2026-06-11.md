### 2. **U.S. May PPI Surges 1.1%, Pushing Annual Wholesale Inflation to 6.5% — Hottest Since 2022**

**Source:** CNBC ([Original Article](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/11/producer-price-index-may-2026-.html)); CBS News ([Original Article](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/ppi-report-today-may-2026-highest-since-2022/))

The May Producer Price Index (PPI) rose 1.1% month-over-month — sharply exceeding the Dow Jones consensus estimate of 0.7% — lifting the year-over-year PPI rate to 6.5%, the highest since November 2022 and a sign that Iran war-driven cost pressures continue to percolate through the supply chain.

Nearly 80% of the monthly gain came from a 2.8% surge in final demand goods prices, including a 10.7% jump in energy and a 23.4% spike in wholesale gasoline, directly tied to Iran war supply disruptions.

Core PPI (ex-food and energy) rose a relatively contained 0.4% m/m, suggesting the inflation shock is still primarily energy-driven — but economists warn that sustained elevated energy costs will increasingly flow through to broader consumer prices.

### 3. **ECB Hikes Rates 25bps for First Time Since 2023, Blaming Iran War Energy Inflation**

**Source:** Bloomberg ([Live Blog](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/live-blog/2026-06-11/ecb-interest-rate-decision-euro-lagarde-press-conference-live-updates)); ECB ([Press Release](https://www.ecb.europa.eu/press/pr/date/2026/html/ecb.mp260611~4d41bd5e83.en.html))

The European Central Bank raised all three key interest rates by 25 basis points — bringing the deposit facility rate to 2.25%, the main refinancing rate to 2.40%, and the marginal lending rate to 2.65%, effective June 17 — marking its first rate hike since 2023.

The decision was driven by Eurozone CPI reaching 3.2% in May, well above the ECB's 2% target, fueled by Iran war-related energy cost increases. The ECB simultaneously revised its 2026 Eurozone GDP growth forecast down to 0.8%, with headline inflation not expected to return to target until 2028.

A Bloomberg opinion column sharply criticized the move as "jumping at old inflation shadows," arguing the ECB risks overtightening into an already fragile European economic recovery.
