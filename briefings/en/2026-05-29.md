### 2. **April PCE Hits Highest Level in Nearly Three Years; Markets Brush It Off**

**Source:** U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis ([Original Article](https://www.bea.gov/news/2026/personal-income-and-outlays-april-2026))

The BEA released April Personal Income and Outlays data on May 28: headline PCE rose 3.8% year-over-year and core PCE (ex-food and energy) rose 3.3% YoY — both the highest readings in nearly three years, well above the Fed's 2% target. On a monthly basis, however, the data showed modest deceleration: headline PCE +0.4% MoM (vs. +0.7% in March) and core +0.2% MoM (vs. +0.3%).

The personal saving rate fell to 2.6% while consumer spending rose $111B (+0.5%), suggesting households are drawing down savings to maintain consumption. Markets largely absorbed the hot print without a meaningful selloff, with investors focused instead on Iran ceasefire optimism and the strong AI earnings backdrop.
