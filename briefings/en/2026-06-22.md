### 2. **Alphabet Plunges 6.7% as Top AI Talent Bolts for Rivals**

**Source:** CNBC ([Original Article](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/22/alphabet-goog-stock-ai-departures.html)) / Bloomberg

Alphabet shares suffered their worst single-day decline in more than a year, falling 6.7%, as two high-profile AI leaders announced departures within the same day. Noam Shazeer — VP of Engineering and co-lead of Gemini, Google's flagship AI model — left for OpenAI. Hours later, John Jumper — a Google DeepMind VP and co-creator of AlphaFold2 who shared the 2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry — announced his departure for Anthropic.

The back-to-back exits sparked fears that Alphabet's AI talent pipeline is fracturing, with its closest rivals now offering equity upside and creative autonomy that Google's corporate structure cannot match. Compounding the selloff: a California court denied Google and YouTube's request for a new trial in a youth-platform addiction lawsuit, escalating legal exposure. Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella's Sunday remarks on AI commoditization further rattled investors already questioning whether Alphabet's $75B annual AI capex can maintain a defensible lead.

SpaceX shares (SPCX) fell another 16.4% on Monday, extending the post-IPO correction to a third consecutive session. From its June 16 high of $201.80, SPCX has now shed approximately 23%, erasing more than $600 billion in market capitalization in just five trading days.

The proximate trigger was SpaceX's announcement of a $20 billion investment-grade bond offering — its first ever IG debt issuance — to finance AI data center expansion. While the bonds themselves are well-covered, the announcement crystallized investor anxiety: the era of trillion-dollar AI capex has arrived, and it will be debt-financed. As Chair Warsh's hawkish stance reprices the cost of that debt higher, the AI infrastructure buildout faces a new valuation stress test it has not encountered before.

**Source:** CNBC / Trading Economics

The net macro effect is disinflationary at the margin — welcome for a Fed wrestling with sticky core PCE — but the relief is not yet fully banked given the fragility of the ceasefire and unresolved nuclear talks.
